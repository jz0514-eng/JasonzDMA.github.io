Jason Zheng is a student at San José State University, majoring in Digital Media Art with a focus on UI/UX design. Growing up heavily influenced by video games and visual media, he became interested in how digital artwork can express emotions and messages that are not possible with words.

With this idea in mind, he aims to connect his creative process to his hobbies. He tries to expand the range of media he could use in his creative process by experimenting with different media such as sewing, painting, and sculpting. This also made him study Digital Media Art, where he could experiment with more media to expand his scope and techniques.

At San José State University, Jason has been experimenting with different media such as 3D modeling, video art, photography, and coding. Through this process, he discovered an interest in photography and UI/UX design. He finds a unique sense of accomplishment when creating a functional code in JavaScript or slowing down to see a different point of view with photography.

Jason's work often explores ways to connect different ideas. He is interested in how digital tools can create interactions between concepts that normally don't appear together. Whether it is coding or capturing a photograph, he aims to create work that feels personal while also engaging with modern digital technology.

Currently, he is exploring how UI/UX principles can change his creative process and how JavaScript can be used to bring more ideas to life.
