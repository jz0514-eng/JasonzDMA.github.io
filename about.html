Bio

Jason Zheng is a student at San José State University, majoring in Digital Media Art with a focus on UI/UX design. Growing up heavily influenced by video games and visual media, he became interested in how digital artwork can express emotions and messages that are not possible with words.

Artist Statement

The goal with my work is to capture or create what I like and what I find interesting. It could be a random idea I come up with, or a random view I came across. I enjoy taking pictures of what I find and use them later as reference photos or use them as inspiration, as long as they are interesting. Because if there wasn’t an interest in it, there wouldn’t be an artist who would want to make something about it, and because of this, the majority of my artwork is personal to me and often contains messages that only someone who knows me would understand.

I also like experimenting with different media to find what works best, as people always say creativity is when you put something unexpected together, and I also found that by experimenting with different medium, it could give me a different point of view of the same topic, for example, photography, before I started doing photography, I rush to my destination, but after I started trying to take a picture and beware of what is around me, I started paying more attention to what is going on, and was able to see a lot more interesting things that I normally skipped.

Being able to expand your perspective is an important skill, in my opinion. by trying out new things, you will be able to push your limits and create work that is unique and different, for example, it can be sewing, by using fabric and stitching your artwork, you will be able to create a unique texture, while it can be challenging as I’m experimenting with a new medium, but it is still important to try, because if you have never tried, you would be able to learn, and you will never know if it will work out, and I also believe that the process of creating is also important, because when you are making something, you are constantly learning and practicing, and sometimes the message of an artwork is within the process, while the results are only an artifact of what's left behind.

In my opinion, being an artist is not just about creating art, but also the journey of self-discovery. By creating artwork that feels personal, you are able to express your feelings and create artwork that represents the life of the artist. And that is my goal with what I create, something that reflects on my past and my dream. Freedom is also a core part of being an artist; the only thing limiting you is your imagination. Being an artist is the journey of trying new things and finding what is fun, and to find what best fits you and represents you the most, and to express those ideas with the artwork you create.
